1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small pieces of text sent by your web browser by a website you visit. A cookie file is stored in your web browser and allows the Service or a third-party to recognize you and make your next visit easier and the Service more useful to you. They are essentially digital memory aids for websites, designed to make your online experience smoother and more personalized. Think of them like tiny flavor profiles that a website remembers about your preferences.

When you visit a website, the website sends a cookie to your computer or mobile device. Your computer stores it in a file located inside your web browser. When you revisit the same website, the website retrieves the cookie from your computer. The information stored in the cookie can be used to track your activity on the website, remember your login details, or customize your experience. This fundamental mechanism allows for a seamless interaction, preventing you from having to re-enter information or preferences repeatedly, much like remembering your favorite snack order at your local munchie spot.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your personal computer or mobile device when you go offline, while session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your web browser. Each type serves a distinct purpose, contributing to different aspects of your browsing experience, from long-term personalization to ensuring secure, single-session interactions. Understanding this distinction is key to comprehending how cookies operate behind the scenes.

They are not programs and do not contain viruses or malware. They are simply text files that contain small amounts of data. This data is often encrypted for security purposes, ensuring that only the originating website can read and interpret the information stored within them. Their primary function is to facilitate communication between your browser and the website, enabling features that would otherwise be cumbersome or impossible.

The use of cookies has become a standard practice across the internet, largely due to their utility in enhancing user experiences. Without cookies, many modern web functionalities, such as shopping carts, personalized dashboards, or even remembering your language preferences, would be significantly more challenging to implement. They are an invisible but integral part of the contemporary web.

It's important to differentiate cookies from other tracking technologies like web beacons or pixel tags, though they often work in conjunction. While cookies store data on your device, web beacons are tiny graphics that help monitor user behavior, often used in emails to track open rates. This policy primarily focuses on cookies but acknowledges the broader ecosystem of digital tracking.

The data within a cookie is typically anonymous and aggregated, meaning it doesn't directly identify you as an individual unless you have explicitly provided personal information to the website. Instead, it identifies your browser or device, allowing for generalized tracking of browsing patterns and preferences. This distinction is crucial for understanding the privacy implications of cookie usage.

Ultimately, cookies serve as a bridge between your current browsing session and your past interactions with a website. They enable websites to "remember" you, not necessarily as a person, but as a consistent user, thereby facilitating a more tailored and efficient online experience. This memory function is what makes many interactive web features possible and enjoyable.

Persistent cookies are crucial for features like "Snack-Saver Mode," where your personalized collection of recipes needs to be retained between different browsing sessions. They ensure that your curated snack library is always available when you return.

4.3. Opting Out of Specific Advertising Cookies

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and the UK, you can visit the European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA) opt-out page: http://www.youronlinechoices.eu/.

For users in the United States, you can visit the Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) opt-out page: http://www.aboutads.info/choices/ or the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) opt-out page: http://optout.networkadvertising.org/?c=1.

It's important to note that if you clear your browser's cookies, you will also remove these opt-out cookies and will need to opt-out again.

Opting out of advertising cookies does not mean you will no longer see advertisements. It simply means that the ads you see will be less relevant to your interests, as they will not be based on your browsing behavior tracked by these specific advertising networks. You will still see generic or contextual ads.

These industry opt-out tools are a valuable resource for users who want to reduce personalized advertising across the web without completely blocking all cookies, which might impact website functionality.

We encourage you to explore these options if you are concerned about targeted advertising. They provide a centralized way to manage your preferences across a wide range of advertising partners.

Remember that these opt-outs are specific to the browser and device you are using. If you use multiple browsers or devices, you will need to opt-out on each one individually to achieve comprehensive coverage.

While Snacktum strives to be transparent about its own cookie usage, the behavior of third-party advertising partners is governed by their own policies and these industry-wide opt-out mechanisms.

In summary, industry opt-out pages offer a way to specifically control targeting and advertising cookies from a broad range of participating companies, providing an additional layer of privacy control beyond browser settings.

5. Cookie Duration and Lifespan

The lifespan of a cookie varies significantly depending on its type and purpose. Understanding how long cookies remain on your device is an important aspect of managing your privacy.

5.1. Session-Based Lifespan

As previously mentioned, session cookies are temporary and are designed to expire as soon as you close your web browser. Their lifespan is limited to the duration of your active browsing session. Once the browser window is closed, these cookies are automatically deleted from your device.

This short lifespan makes them ideal for maintaining session-specific information, such as remembering items in a shopping cart, maintaining login status during a single visit, or tracking your progress through a multi-step form. They do not retain any information after your session ends.

For example, if you are logged into your Snacktum account to access "Snack-Saver Mode," a session cookie ensures you remain logged in as you navigate between different recipe pages. Once you close your browser, that login session cookie is gone, requiring you to log in again on your next visit (unless a persistent "remember me" cookie is also used).

Their ephemeral nature contributes to their lower privacy risk profile, as they do not store long-term data about your browsing habits. They are purely functional for the immediate interaction.

This automatic deletion mechanism provides a clean slate for each new browsing session, ensuring that temporary data does not accumulate on your device unnecessarily.

In summary, session cookies have a very short lifespan, tied directly to your active browsing session, and are automatically deleted upon browser closure, serving only to facilitate immediate site functionality.

5.2. Persistent Lifespan

Persistent cookies, in contrast, have a defined expiration date set by the website that creates them. This lifespan can range from a few minutes or hours to several months or even years. They remain on your device until their expiration date is reached or until you manually delete them.

These cookies are used for long-term tracking and remembering preferences across multiple visits. For example, a persistent cookie might remember your consent choice for our cookie banner for a year, preventing you from seeing it on every visit.

Analytics cookies are often persistent, allowing us to track user behavior over extended periods, such as understanding how many times a user returns to the "Snack Recipes" section over a month or a year. This helps us analyze long-term engagement trends.

Advertising cookies are typically persistent, enabling ad networks to build a profile of your interests over time and across various websites. Their long lifespan allows for more sophisticated targeting and retargeting campaigns.

The specific duration of each persistent cookie is determined by the third party that sets it (for third-party cookies) or by Snacktum (for first-party persistent cookies). This duration is chosen based on the purpose of the cookie.

You have the power to override this set lifespan by manually clearing your browser's cookies at any time. This will delete all persistent cookies stored on your device.

While persistent cookies offer significant benefits in terms of personalization and convenience, their long-term nature is why privacy regulations often require explicit consent for their use.

In summary, persistent cookies have a predefined lifespan, allowing them to remain on your device for extended periods to remember preferences, facilitate long-term analytics, and enable targeted advertising across multiple sessions.
